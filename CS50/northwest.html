Climate

The North West is very exposed to wet weather, because of the high incline areas and particular exposure to the Atlantic ocean. This rain creates a very wet cliate that is perfect for abundant plant growth. Here are our suggestions for plants that are easy to cultiate and maintain in this climate!!

Flowers

SOURTHERN MARSH ORCHID don't mind the name; these are likely the most relilient orchid and can thrive in drier soil as well as soil. Not too tricky to look after.


ROSEBAY WILLOWHERB the North-West is covered in these! Even if you don't plant them, don't be surprised if they make their way into your garden.


HONEYSUCKLE easy to cultivate and fantastic for wildlife. You may not like bees, but we have to keep them alive somehow.


MEADOWSWEET grows best in damper soil or near water sources. Features gorgeoud white blooms and a sweet scent.

Moss

This may seem like an odd choice for a garden, but moss produces massive amounts of oxygen and is great for the envirment. Find some mossy rocks in your nearby rural areas and use them in your garden!

MARSH HAIR MOSS> the most common in the UK and can grow in boggy areas

ORDINARY MOSS the easiest possible moss to grow, it can even be found in some lawns!

Shrubs

BILBERRIES very easy to look after, and can be used to make jams or sauces when they become ripe in summer. They appear similar to blueberries, and feature pink flowers in June

DOG-ROSEbeautiful pink petal in summer, very easy to cultivate.Rosehips are a good source of vitamin C, and can be collected and used to make sweet, rosehip syrups and jellies.

COMMON HAWTHORN feature white blossoms in spring and small red fruit in autumn. a rich habitat for wildlife, from hawthorn shield bugs and yellowhammers to slow worms that shelter in the thickets.
